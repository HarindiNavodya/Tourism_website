best Places in
Sri Lanka




wildlife

Udawalawe National Park

This large wildlife park is in the southern part of the country.Visitors to the park can see elephants, as there's a large population of them, along with crocodiles, water buffalo, and more. In fact, it's one of the best places in the world to see elephants in the wild outside of Africa.

Bundala National Park

Bundala National Park is home to a wide range of terrain including scrub jungle, salt pans, sand dunes and wetlands which allows for a rich diversity of wildlife. The park’s beaches play habitat to all five species of turtle who lay their eggs here.Bundala is one of Sri Lanka’s finest places for birdwatching.

Minneriya National Park

Minneriya National Park is one of the smallest parks in Sri Lanka.Mammals found in Minneriya include deer, purple-faced langur and macaque monkeys, sloth bears and about 20 leopards, plus there is a wonderful range of migratory and indigenous birds.

Hikkaduwa Marine National Park

The Hikkaduwa National Park in the southern province is one of the marine national park in Sri Lanka. Although the southern coastline is home to many coral reefs, the reefs in Hikkaduwa are the most diverse and accessible and offer some of the country’s best snorkelling and diving opportunities.

Adventure-Tourism

Kitulgala

Located south of Kandy, the little town of Kitulgala is the gateway to the Kelaniya Gangas rapids and the ideal place for you to ride the waves of this powerful river. Hence, it is indeed a great spot for beginners as well as experienced rafters alike. After getting a dose of adrenaline, you can relax and go on hikes through the jungle, go bird watching, and also embark on waterfall trekking trails.

Hikkaduwa

There is nothing quite as fascinating as the underwater world, and deep sea diving takes you to the depths of this realm so you can explore and experience its magic. If you love deep sea diving, one of the best adventure places in Sri Lanka is off the coast of Hikkaduwa, which is one of the South East Coasts most popular towns. It is one of the best places to go deep sea diving and explore the secret life of the marine world.

Midigama

Located past Galle in the southern part of the island, Midigama is one of the best surf beaches here. With sandy beaches stretching out for miles, it is the perfect place to ride the waves or lie back and watch others give it a go. Keep in mind that the waters of Midigama are best for experienced surfers only as there are a few reefs that cause the waves to jump high.

Kalpitiya

Kalpitiya provides the perfect background for you to enjoy this exciting water sport. If you are a freestyler, then the Kalpitiya Lagoon is the ideal place for you. If you are new to the sport, you will find a few schools that have trained and experienced experts who can show you the ropes. And for those whose interest lies in kitesurfing, Kalpitiya serves as one of the top adventure places in Sri Lanka.

Cultural Heritage

Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura is one of the world heritage site and the place gained importance only after the coming of the Bodhi tree which is known as the tree of enlightenment. The cutting of this tree was brought to Sri Lanka by Sanghamitta and is also one of the famous places to see in Sri Lanka.

Pollonnaruwa

This is an important ancient city ruin site, another one of the country's World Heritage Sites. Pollonnaruwa was Sri Lanka's second capital city, after Anuradhapura, thriving about a thousand years ago.

Kandy

Kandy, the second largest city in Sri Lanka, is the gateway to the Central Highlands and its tropical plantations that grow both tea and rubber. If you’re driving from Colombo, you’ll pass by rubber plantations on a road that is considered one of the country’s most scenic. The last capital of the ancient kingdoms, Kandy is home to the Temple of the Tooth Relic.

Sigiriya

Sigiriya is an ancient rock fortress and castle/palace ruin situated in the central Matale District of Sri Lanka, surrounded by the remains of an extensive network of gardens, reservoirs, and other structures. It is a popular tourist destination, also known for its ancient paintings (frescos), very similar to those in the Ajanta Caves of India. The Sigiraya was built during the reign of King Kassapa, and it is one of the seven World Heritage Sites of Sri Lanka.

eco-tourism

Sinharaja Rain Forest

Sinharaja Rain Forest is a world heritage site and major eco tourism destination, which can also be described as a Tropical Lowland Rainforest or Tropical Wet Evergreen Forest . Whatever its - technical' name, it is undoubtedly a rich treasure trove of nature with a great diversity of habitats and a vast repository of Sri Lanka's endemic species found no where else in the world.

Knuckles Mountains

The KNUCKLES MOUNTAIN RANGE (so-called because its appearance resembles a set of knuckles in a closed fist) is a major eco tourism venue of Sri Lanka and has been declared a conservation area, now known as the KNUCKLES NATIONAL HERITAGE AND WILDERNESS AREA.

Bambarakanda Waterfalls

The tallest waterfall yet in Sri Lanka which boasts a height of 789 feet. Located in a dense forest around Bandarawela which is very difficult to get through but still is a well worth journey. Still if you are unable to make the special trip to this giant waterfall you can catch a glimse of it on the way to Haputale.

Bakers Waterfall

Another waterfall located at Hoton Plains and is also one of the most beautiful waterfalls in the country. It is a cascading water fall with not one but several falls. However, it is a very dangerous waterfall to bath in. It is about 20 metres high and is named after a well known explorer in the name of Sir Samuel Baker. The fall originates from a tank called Belihul Oya. The scenary surrounding it will definitely take your breath-away.
